Objective
Design a clean, high-efficiency Invoice & Operations Management iOS App with a single-source-of-truth dashboard and a unified Purchase workflow. The system must prioritize speed, minimal data entry, instant visibility of liabilities/receivables, and operational clarity across purchases, sales, job work, and purchase orders.

1. Dashboard (Home Screen)
The dashboard is the control center. It must surface only decision-critical metrics—no clutter.
Primary Sections (Clickable Cards)
1.	Purchase
2.	Sales
3.	Job Work
4.	Purchase Order
Each card opens its respective module. No sub-navigation from the dashboard beyond these four.

Key Financial & Operational Highlights (Top Summary Area)
Display as high-contrast numeric tiles:
1.	Total Pending Payable
o	Aggregate unpaid purchase invoices
o	Existing breakdowns remain:
	Upcoming
	Discrepancies
2.	Total Pending Receivable
o	Aggregate unpaid sales invoices
o	Mandatory sub-breakdown:
	Number of invoices pending
	Amount due within 15 days
	Amount due within 45 days
3.	Job Work Exposure
o	Total Labour Charges Payable
o	Total Zinc Pending
	Quantity-based (MT/KG)
	Optional value mapping if rates exist
All dashboard metrics must be:
•	Auto-calculated
•	Real-time
•	Tappable → deep-link to filtered records

2. Purchase Module (Single Unified Section) – ALEADY ALL OF THE CURRENT SECTION

3. Sales Module
Structurally identical to Purchase, but inverted for outbound flow.
Sales = Purchase Logic × Customer Perspective
•	Sales invoices instead of purchase invoices
•	Customers instead of vendors
•	Receivables instead of payables
Key Capabilities
•	Customer search (same strength as vendor search)
•	Sales invoice aging
•	Auto-calculated receivables
•	Direct linkage to dashboard:
o	Pending invoices count
o	Within 15 days
o	Within 45 days
Consistency between Purchase and Sales is mandatory to reduce cognitive load.

4. Job Work Module
Dedicated module to track external processing costs and material exposure.
Core Tracking
1.	Labour Charges
o	Vendor-wise
o	Job-wise
o	Total payable reflected on dashboard
2.	Zinc Tracking
o	Zinc issued – WILL BE FETECHED FROM JOB WORK CHALLAN – TO WHICH VENDOR IT IS DOING, WHEN IT IS GOING, CHALLAN NO., KGS ISSUED, GST NUMBER. 
o	Zinc consumed (WE WILL ISSUE FORMULA – DIFFERENT VENDOR’S HAVE DIFFERENT % PER TON FOR DIFFERENT THICKNESS OF MATERIAL) – “% PER TON PER THICKNESS DATA WILL BE PRE FEEDED, AUTOMATICALLY FETCHING FROM JOB WORK CHALLAN WILL BE – WHICH VENDOR IS OUR MATERIAL IS GOING, WHAT MATERIAL IS GOING, WHAT THICKNESS IS GOING, WHAT KGS ARE GOING”
o	Zinc pending (balance)
o	Quantity-first design (cost optional but supported)
Dashboard tiles must directly reflect Job Work totals.

5. Purchase Order Module
Purely order-centric. No invoice noise.
Functions
•	Create and manage purchase orders
•	Track:
o	Ordered quantity
o	Received quantity
o	Pending quantity
•	Link purchase invoices back to one or multiple purchase orders
•	Status visibility:
o	Open
o	Partially fulfilled
o	Closed

Design & UX Principles (Non-Negotiable)
•	Single-tap access to all critical data
•	Minimal typing; maximum reuse of existing data
•	Strong search everywhere (vendor, customer, invoice)
•	Dashboard drives behavior, not reports
•	All numbers must be reconcilable via tap-through

Sales Module (Mirror of Purchase)
The Sales section must be a structural and functional clone of Purchase, with terminology and logic inverted for outbound transactions. When the user taps Sales from the dashboard, this module opens.

1. Customer Management (Mirror of Vendor Management)
•	Central customer list
•	Global search option (mandatory) Search by:
o	Customer name
o	GST
o	Phone
o	Sales invoice reference
•	Customer profile view displays:
o	Total outstanding receivable
o	Number of pending sales invoices
o	Last transaction date
Behavior, layout, and interaction must be identical to Vendor Management in Purchase.

2. Sales Invoice Handling (Mirror of Purchase Invoices)
•	Add / view / edit sales invoices
•	Invoice attributes (identical structure):
o	Customer
o	Invoice number
o	Invoice date
o	Due date
o	Invoice value
o	Tax breakup
o	Linked sales order (if applicable)
•	Status indicators:
o	Paid
o	Partially paid
o	Pending
o	Disputed
All validation, flows, and UI patterns must match Purchase.

3. Receivable Intelligence (Mirror of Payable Intelligence)
•	Auto-calculated receivables
•	Aging buckets (identical logic):
o	Due
o	Overdue
o	Upcoming
•	Dashboard-linked drill-down:
o	Total pending receivable
o	Pending invoice count
o	Amount due within 15 days
o	Amount due within 45 days
No custom logic beyond what exists in Purchase.

4. Discrepancy Handling (Mirror)
•	Flag discrepancies on:
o	Quantity mismatch
o	Rate mismatch
o	Tax mismatch
Discrepancy resolution workflow must be identical to Purchase.

Dashboard Integration (Sales)
•	Sales tile opens Sales module
•	Dashboard Total Pending Receivable pulls data strictly from Sales
•	Breakdown logic mirrors Purchase payable logic, inverted

Implementation Rule (Critical)
If a feature exists in Purchase, it must exist in Sales in the same position, with the same interaction cost, and the same calculation logic—only the business noun changes.
No additional features. No simplification. No deviation.

Final Check Constraint
•	A user trained on Purchase must be able to use Sales without learning anything new.
